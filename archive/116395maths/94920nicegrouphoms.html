Kevin Buzzard (Mar 12 2020 at 12:14):

Say f:G→Hf:G\to Hf:G→H is a group homomorphism. If AAA is a subgroup of GGG and BBB is a subgroup of HHH then one can ask that fff has the "nice" property that f(A)⊆Bf(A)\subseteq Bf(A)⊆B -- fff is "preserving the subgroups". Does this predicate (which of course depends on AAA and BBB, so should really be called "AAA-BBB-nice") have a name? If fff has this nice property then it induces a group homomorphism A→BA\to BA→B and in the situation where everything is abelian it also induces a group homomorphism G/A→H/BG/A\to H/BG/A→H/B. These induced homomorphisms are functorial in the sense that if fff is the identity and A=BA=BA=B then the induced maps on subs and quotients are also the identity, and if fff and ggg are nice then so is g∘fg\circ fg∘f and furthermore the induced maps on subgroups and quotients also can be computed by composition.

I am trying a crazy experiment of bundling subquotients of abelian groups, and a bundled subquotient of GGG is a pair A1⊆A2⊆GA_1\subseteq A_2\subseteq GA1​⊆A2​⊆G of subgroups. One gets now a new notion of "nice", which says f(A1)⊆B1f(A_1)\subseteq B_1f(A1​)⊆B1​ and f(A2)⊆B2f(A_2)\subseteq B_2f(A2​)⊆B2​. If fff is "(A1,A2)(A_1,A_2)(A1​,A2​)-(B1,B2)(B_1,B_2)(B1​,B2​)-nice" then it induces a group homomorphism A2/A1→B2/B1A_2/A_1\to B_2/B_1A2​/A1​→B2​/B1​ which is the identity if Ai=BiA_i=B_iAi​=Bi​; the construction also sends composition to composition. This is presumably some basic concept but I don't know what to call it (and hence I'm having trouble naming my functions)

Given a short exact sequence of complexes one gets a long exact sequence of cohomology groups. It's time we had this in Lean in some form, and I'm just kicking around trying to figure out various ways of doing it. Do you or @Reid Barton have a clear idea about how this should be done?
