Scott Morrison (Jun 12 2019 at 01:23):

Dear all, but particularly the mathematicians! (@Kevin Buzzard, @Patrick Massot, @Johan Commelin, @Neil Strickland, @Reid Barton)

The annual call for proposals at Banff has just come out https://www.birs.ca/announcements/2019-06-06/2021-Call-for-Proposals

I've successfully organised a number of conferences there in the past, and I wonder if we might try putting something in...

Scott Morrison (Jun 12 2019 at 01:23):

They do a great job of handling all the logistical work, which is great, and they cover accommodation and local expenses for all participants, so it can be a great way to get people together.

Scott Morrison (Jun 12 2019 at 01:25):

  • It's nice to have meetings to get everyone together to talk and work...!
  • I think it's good to try to push formalisation and interactive theorem proving into visible places, and organising conferences is part of this.

Scott Morrison (Jun 12 2019 at 01:27):

Maybe we'd be shut out immediately, but I don't think it's obvious. We would certainly want to invite people from CS (and potentially even other fields, as at Big Proof), but I think if the organisers are primarily mathematicians we could make a strong case that something new and exciting is happening.

Scott Morrison (Jun 12 2019 at 01:28):

Proposing a workshop at BIRS is not a huge amount of work --- from memory you end up writing a 5-10 page document for the proposal, and then if you're accepted, being an organiser is pretty easy.

Johan Commelin (Jun 12 2019 at 04:48):

Sounds like an exciting idea!

Kevin Buzzard (Jun 12 2019 at 06:05):

I've also organised a Banff gig and indeed my experience was also that it was pretty easy. My memory is that you're only allowed to invite about 15 people though -- although perhaps we went for some smaller option or something?

Scott Morrison (Jun 12 2019 at 07:04):

There are two versions: the 21 person or 42 person workshops.

Neil Strickland (Jun 13 2019 at 08:46):
