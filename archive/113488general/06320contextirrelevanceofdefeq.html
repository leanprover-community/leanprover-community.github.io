Mario Carneiro (Feb 29 2020 at 03:04):

Another good question in the same area. Obviously the "natural type" of eee does not have any free variables not in eee itself, but it is possible for defeq terms to have different sets of free variables because of proof irrelevance.

For example, consider the proofs A:U⊢refl  A:A=AA:U\vdash refl\;A: A = AA:U⊢reflA:A=A and A:U,h:⊥⊢rec⊥  (A=A)  h:A=AA:U,h:\bot\vdash rec_{\bot}\; (A=A)\; h:A=AA:U,h:⊥⊢rec⊥​(A=A)h:A=A. By proof irrelevance, these two are defeq in the context A:U,h:⊥A:U,h:\botA:U,h:⊥. So you could have A:U,x:A,h:⊥⊢x:cast(rec⊥  (A=A)  h)AA:U,x:A,h:\bot\vdash x: cast (rec_{\bot}\; (A=A)\; h) AA:U,x:A,h:⊥⊢x:cast(rec⊥​(A=A)h)A (where the cast is defeq to AAA).
